# Key Insights

## Trend Analysis
- Global forest area declined steadily from 1992 to 2021.

## Comparative Analysis
- Russian Federation, Brazil, Canada, and the United States hold the largest forest areas.

## Forest Loss Anlaysis
- Brazil experienced the largest forest loss since 1990.

## Correlation Anlysis
- GDP growth and forest area showed a weak relationship, indicating that forest loss is influenced by multiple economic and policy factors.

## Forest Gain Analysis
- Some countries showed positive forest area growth between 1990 and 2021.
- Forest gain trends suggest the impact of conservation and reforestation policies.
- The analysis highlights that forest management outcomes vary significantly across countries.
